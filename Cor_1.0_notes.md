### 2026-01-09
#### Onboarding Notes
- Project number: 21062.
- Midmark 800 Digital Vital Signs Device notes: manual entry for weight, height, BMI, RR, and pain; measures BP, pulse rate, SpO2, and temperature; battery or cord powered; rechargeable Li-ion battery; two stacked USB-C ports; two Ethernet ports; multiple wall mounts; side connections for Masimo SET rainbow and likely blood pressure.
- Cart notes: no electronics except passthrough USB-C power for the vital signs device.
- Model notes: four models; one without SpO2; one with Masimo and one with Nellcor SpO2; one with Masimo SpO2 plus automated respiration rate.
- Testing notes: lifecycle testing focused on NIBP; 7-year life target with about 63k readings over 2 months; check functionality and drift after life testing; partially automated; about 30% complete as of 2026-01-06; sample size of 3.
- Compliance testing notes: most testing can be done onsite, including chemical, environmental, and leakage current; EMC is usually done at Intertek Lexington but was done in Irvine for this project.
- PCB assembly/test notes: ATE1 is the automated functional bed-of-nails tester; there is a separate NIBP tester.
- Electronics ownership notes: Midmark owns the main board, connector board, and NIBP board; the SpO2 board is licensed from Masimo or Nellcor.
- Major onboarding issues: 510(k) deficiency #11 asked for a clinical study for blood-pressure-inferred pulse rate on NIBP; incorrect threaded inserts were used in the latest build; screen flicker occurs after software update and currently requires a full power cycle; 8 kV air discharge causes touch latency; NIBP leak failures were occurring during PCB assembly/test; pulse rate could display even when BP was bad; the requirement/IFU says audio should play when connecting or sending to EMR but that was not implemented.
- Software/build notes: current release was v1.1.5; intended next release with screen-flicker fix and minor changes was v1.1.6; VB4 built 15 pieces on 2026-01-12 to 2026-01-13; VB5 built 39 pieces on 2026-01-20 to 2026-01-21; VB6 was the Q Run planned for 2026-02-25 to 2026-03-03; VB7 was the commercial build.
- Cor2.0 note: technical feasibility work on wireless connectivity and auto-connect devices was already underway.
- Risk notes: battery life, touchscreen issue, life-test scope, NIBP leaks, software/firmware bugs, and supplier quality.
- Program history: Midmark had been buying OEM equipment from Zoe and branding it as Midmark without going through a 510(k); Zoe exited the vitals business with two years notice; Midmark pulled the design in house as Cor 1.0 without receiving Zoe IP; target launch had been July 2025 with commitment by end of 2025; development moved from Irvine to Cincinnati in July 2025.

#### Main Schematic
- Connector PCBA interface notes said there are NIBP signals present there, which seemed unexpected because they would normally be expected on the NIBP PCBA.
- VEXT is 12V from the AC adapter.

- TI LSF0102 level translator is used for SMBUS.
- LTC2955I-2 handles push-button detection and power on/off and controls a PFET from VSYS to VSYS_SW.
- Power architecture generates 12V, 5V, and 3.3V from VSYS, with voltage monitoring.
- OnSemi MC34161 monitors UV/OV on 5V and 12V.
- MCP23008 I2C GPIO expander reads UV/OV and PGOOD status lines.
- MAX20404 converters are used for 3.3V and 5V rails from VSYS_SW, and MAX25240 provides 12V from VSYS_SW.
- TLP2745 optocoupler passes serial data from the temperature interface to the SOM.
- SpO2 isolation uses ADuM242E for reset and UART isolation plus TPS79901 and OnSemi NCP718ASN330T1G LDO stages on the isolated side.
- Scale chair isolation uses a Wurth 4-channel digital isolator, SOM-to-chair UART, presence signaling, and an isolated RS-232 transceiver.
